About Us

Love gardening? Want a hands-on service experience?

Volunteer in the Campus Garden; we welcome students, faculty, staff, and community members. All are welcome, no gardening experience is required.

FAQs

What should I wear?

You should wear clothes you do not mind getting dirty, comfortable shoes, weather appropriate pants or shorts, sunscreen, hats, sunglasses on sunny days. We also recomment that you bring a reusable water bottle.

What type of activities do volunteers participate in?

  • Building structures for the garden -- such as hoop houses or garden beds
  • Planting seeds and seedlings
  • Harvesting produce
  • Turning the compost
  • Watering and weeding

    • What is grown in the garden?

    In mid-to-late spring, the garden focus on summer crops such as eggplant, tomatoes, squash, watermelon, and strawberries. In the fall, mostly leafy plants that can withstand the unpredictable weather patterns of the fall and early winter months are planted.
